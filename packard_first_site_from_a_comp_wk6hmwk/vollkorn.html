VOLLKORN

YEAR CREATED: 2005

DESIGNERS: Friedrich Althausen

SERIF

TYPEFACE HISTORY

Vollkorn came into being as the designer's first type designing attempt. He published the Regular in 2005 under a Creative-Commons-License. The counter collapsed two years later after it had been downloaded thousands of times and used for web and print. Vollhorn is intended to be a quiet, modest and well working text face for bread and butter use. Unlike its examples in the book faces from the renaissance until today, it has dark and meaty serifs and a bouncing and healthy look. It might be used as body type as well as for headlines or titles. 'Vollkorn' (pronounced 'Follkorn') is German for 'wholemeal' which refers to the old term 'Brotschrift'. It stood for the small fonts for every day use in hand setting times.

DESIGNER BIOGRAPHY

In 2003 Friedrich started his studies of communication design at the Bauhaus-University in Weimar, Germany. Prior to that he had no idea where the small typefaces came from. After some semesters focusing on illustration he got into the typography course of Alex Branczyk's and met colleagues like Yanone, Georg Seifert and Ralf Hermann. Friedrich learned to identify typefaces along the way. The following semester he started to draw his first serious typeface, assisted by Prof. Jay Rutherford.
